Current Site Overview

Webflow site for Lienhard Schuhe, a Swiss handmade footwear maker based in Thun, in business since 1952. Homepage is built from stacked content blocks — brand intro, "Über Uns" (About), "Produktion" (Production), and "Unser Laden" (Our Store) — followed by a dark footer with navigation and contact details.

Strengths

  • Minimalist, legible sans-serif typography — reads as modern and clean.
  • High-quality product photography, shot cleanly on white — shows off leather detail and craftsmanship well.
  • Clear, straightforward product information.

Weaknesses

  • Missing "brand breath": for a handmade-since-1952 heritage brand, the craftsmanship story — the workshop, the natural leather, the traditional technique — doesn't come through.
  • Monotonous homepage layout: content blocks (About, Shop, Production) stack linearly and evenly, with no large visual highlights (full-bleed banners, short workshop videos) to break up the rhythm.
  • Basic shopping flow: filters are minimal — no visual filter by size or price; shoppers have to open each product individually just to see available sizes.
  • No Featured Products / Bestsellers section on the homepage — a significant gap for an e-commerce homepage.
  • Header has no search.
  • Mobile menu needs work — current mobile nav reads as cluttered/unpolished:

Two Implementation Options

Two directions are quoted below so the client can choose.

Option A

Replicate

Clone the current site's layout and structure (navigation, homepage sections, product/collection page layout, footer) onto Shopify using the Ella theme's default layout as the build foundation (see Section 3 for theme details — same no-cost license applies here); migrate products, categories, and site content from Webflow to Shopify. A faithful, working equivalent of the current site on Shopify — not a new visual direction.

Effort45 working hours
Timeline10 business days
Rate20–100h bracket → $30/hr
Total$1,350
Option B

Redesign

New homepage and site design built around a "Quiet Luxury + Heritage Craftsmanship" direction: generous white space, high-quality workshop photography, close-up material shots, elegant typography, and a storyline that carries the brand's heritage through the shopping journey — a better fit for Lienhard's positioning and customer base than a trend-led "fashion" look.

Recommended theme: Ella (HaloThemes, Shopify OS 2.0/3.0) — a flexible, multipurpose theme with drag-and-drop, nestable sections that suit the Quiet Luxury + Heritage Craftsmanship direction well: custom hero video/slider, image-gallery blocks for the heritage strip, featured-collection sections, and multi-language support if ever needed for the Swiss market. Normally a $89 premium theme on ThemeForest, but included at no license cost to the client through our team's existing partnership with HaloThemes.

Effort45h dev + 20h design = 65 working hours
Timeline14 business days
Rate20–100h bracket → $30/hr
Total$1,950

Option B — Recommended Homepage Section Structure

1. Hero Section

Full-screen slider, or a short 5–8 second looped video of the workshop (close-up shots of the shoemaking process).

2. Heritage strip

A brand statement paired with a row of close-up black-and-white craftsmanship photos (stitching, cutting, polishing).

3. Split banner

Traditional vs. modern styling side by side.

4. Featured Products / Bestsellers

Currently missing from the homepage entirely; a standard e-commerce essential.

5. Produktion (Production)

Redesigned version of the existing section, carrying more of the workshop/craftsmanship visual story.

Additional UX fixes included in the redesign

  • Header search — add a search icon to the header, alongside a store/map-location icon linking through to the "Unser Laden" section.
  • Mobile menu redesign — replace the current cluttered mobile nav with a cleaner treatment.
  • Visual product filters — add filtering by size, price, etc. on collection/shop pages, instead of requiring a click into each product.
  • Featured Products / Bestsellers block on the homepage (see item 4 above).

Notes & things to confirm with the client

  • Design references: external sites (Velasca, Carmina Shoemaker, Crockett & Jones, John Lobb) shown above are used purely as style/layout references to communicate the intended direction — not to be copied verbatim.
  • Hero treatment (Option B): confirm whether the client wants a static full-screen slider or a short looped workshop video — video requires either source footage from the client or a newly produced/stock clip if none exists.
  • Store info: confirm current opening hours and any additional details to include in the "Unser Laden" section.
  • Footer direction (Option B): confirm which of the two footer references (centered-logo vs. dark-luxury) fits the brand better, or whether to design a third option.
  • Theme license: Ella is used as the build foundation for both options and is provided at no license cost via our team's partnership with HaloThemes — the client doesn't need to purchase it separately either way.
  • Shopify subscription, payment gateway/shipping setup: standard scope notes — the Shopify plan is subscribed to directly by the client; payment gateway and shipping carrier setup handled by the client's own team for credential confidentiality (a setup guide can be provided).
