Current Site Overview

32 Spirits Artisan Textiles — a Brooklyn-based, Laos-handmade luxury textile brand (napkins, placemats, throws, runners, etc.), currently on Squarespace.

Strengths

  • Branding is excellent — the site's standout strength.
  • Soft, cohesive color palette without overuse of color — reads as premium.
  • Elegant typography that fits the brand's positioning.
  • High-quality, artistic photography that communicates product value well.
  • Whitespace is used well — airy layout, feels luxurious.
  • Overall a Luxury-style site focused on visual/emotional experience over hard-selling.

Gaps noted during review

  • Hero banner has no title, subtitle, or CTA button (also a missed SEO opportunity).
  • Header could be tightened: logo runs a little large, no search icon, "Login" is a plain text link rather than an icon.
  • Homepage feels short — the footer appears too soon, cutting the emotional momentum right after it builds.
  • Motion/interaction is minimal — the site reads as fairly static.
  • Footer isn't clearly segmented into sections.
  • Collection pages have no filters; product cards don't surface price inline.

Phase 2 — Design & UX Improvements

Optional, recommended

Covers the design/UX suggestions identified during review, organized by area.

Hero / Banner

  • Add a title, subtitle, and a button link to the hero banner (also helps SEO).
  • Alternative direction: replace the static hero with a short 8–10 second video of the hand-weaving process, materials, or production space, to boost authenticity and emotional impact — video only needs a short title + one small CTA to a collection.
  • If staying with a static image: minimize on-banner text (title + button only) so the photography carries the emotion.
  • Increase hero height for a stronger first impression on landing.

Header

  • Shrink the logo slightly to add breathing room and better balance.
  • Add a search icon.
  • Convert the "Login" text link into an account icon for a cleaner, more modern header.

Motion & Interaction

Add subtle effects: fade-in on appearance, slow zoom on images, image reveal on scroll, gentle hover-lift on images/product cards, smoother transitions between sections. Keep animation subtle — too much undercuts the luxury feel.

Homepage layout & pacing

  • Keep the current section layout, or try an offset left–right block arrangement (one side sitting slightly lower than the other).
  • Enlarge product imagery to emphasize the visual experience; shrink the CTA buttons so attention stays on the imagery.
  • Homepage currently feels short — add one more product/lookbook/story section to extend the journey before the footer appears.
  • Add an Instagram feed/section — near-essential for this style of brand.

Footer

Split into 3 clearly separated segments (brand/nav, tagline/location, social):

Product card & collection page

  • Keep the current product card layout; consider surfacing price directly on the card so shoppers don't need to open the product page just to see it.
  • Add a gentle hover effect on product cards.
  • Add collection filters (price, color, etc.) — not in a sidebar; a filter button in the top corner (left or right) of the collection opens a popup filter panel instead.

Product gallery & product page

Notes & things to confirm with the client

  • Wholesale password protection: recommend Lockgate – Password Page + Lock Pages, whose free plan covers locking one page — fits the single Wholesale page in this scope (its paid tiers add customer-tag/login-based access if the client later wants multiple gated pages). App installation and basic password setup are included in Phase 1 at no extra cost. Configuring the specific access rules (who's authorized, tags/criteria) is the client's own team's responsibility by default — if the client wants our team to configure custom rules on their behalf instead, that's additional scope, not yet estimated, to be quoted once the specific rules are confirmed.
  • Photography: new white-background product photos and any updated homepage imagery are assumed to be supplied by the client (or shot separately) — this proposal covers implementation, not photography production, unless otherwise scoped.
  • Design references: images shown above are used purely as style/layout references to communicate the intended direction — not to be copied verbatim.
  • Theme, Shopify subscription, payment gateway/shipping setup: standard scope notes — theme and Shopify plan are subscribed to/purchased directly by the client; payment gateway and shipping carrier setup handled by the client's own team for credential confidentiality (a setup guide can be provided).
